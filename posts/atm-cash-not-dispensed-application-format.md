### Why Do ATMs Fail to Dispense Cash Despite Account Debits?

Understanding why an ATM fails helps you present a clearer picture when drafting your complaint letter. An ATM transaction involves real-time communication between four distinct entities: the ATM kiosk machine, the acquiring bank (the bank that owns the ATM), the National Financial Switch (NFS managed by NPCI), and the issuing bank (your home bank).

Common technical grounds for cash non-dispensation include:

1. **Host Switch Timeout:** The connection between the ATM host server and your bank's central core banking solution (CBS) drops right after authorization, causing the money to be debited digitally while the dispenser fails to release cash.
2. **Mechanical Dispenser Jams:** Physical bills get stuck in the note-presenter mechanism inside the machine due to damaged, folded, or sticky banknotes.
3. **Sensor Errors (Purge Bin Redirection):** If the ATM's internal optical sensor fails to detect cash moving into the tray within a split second, the machine retracts the cash into an internal security box (Purge Bin) for safety, yet records the transaction as completed.
4. **Power / Network Fluctuation:** Micro-interruptions in the local internet connectivity or power supply during the exact second of physical dispensing.

### Immediate Action Plan: 5 Steps to Take at the ATM Kiosk

The moment cash fails to come out of the ATM, do not panic or leave the kiosk immediately. Follow these 5 steps to collect bulletproof evidence:

* **Step 1: Wait for 2 Minutes:** Stay in front of the machine for at least 120 seconds. Sometimes, slow network processing delays the dispenser by a minute. Ensure no one else enters the kiosk while the transaction is resolving.
* **Step 2: Collect & Preserve the Transaction Slip:** If the ATM prints a receipt, keep it safely. Look for critical details on the slip: **ATM ID**, **Transaction Reference/RRN Number**, **Date**, **Time**, and the **Response Code**.
* **Step 3: Take Photographs / Video:** If no receipt is printed, use your smartphone to take a clear photograph of the ATM screen showing the error message, the ATM ID (usually printed on a sticker on the top corner of the machine), and the location.
* **Step 4: Note the Exact Details:** Note down the exact time, location, ATM terminal number, and whether it was your home bank's ATM or a third-party bank's ATM.
* **Step 5: Call Customer Care Instantly:** Call your bank's official toll-free customer helpline immediately to register an initial complaint ticket and get a **Complaint Reference Number**.

### RBI Guidelines on Failed ATM Transactions (The 5-Day Auto-Reversal Rule)

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) issued landmark circulars regarding failed transactions to ensure bank accountability. Here is what every Indian bank account holder must know:

#### 1. The T+5 Auto-Reversal Window
According to RBI guidelines, banks are mandated to reverse failed ATM transactions and re-credit the debited amount to the customer's account automatically within **T+5 calendar days** (where 'T' is the date of the transaction).

#### 2. Rs. 100 Per Day Compensation Rule
If your home bank fails to credit the debited amount back to your account within **5 calendar days** after the failed transaction date, the bank is legally obligated to pay you compensation at the rate of **Rs. 100 per day for every day of delay**, starting from the 6th day until the money is credited.

> **Key Rule to Remember:** You are entitled to the Rs. 100/day compensation **automatically**, provided you lodge a formal complaint with your bank within 30 days of the transaction.

| Transaction Status | Statutory Timeline | Bank's Financial Liability |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Auto-Reversal Window** | Within 5 Days (T+5) | Full refund of debited cash. |
| **Delayed Reversal (Day 6 onwards)** | Beyond 5 Days | Full refund + **Rs. 100 per day penalty** paid to customer. |
| **Complaint Filing Limit** | Within 30 Days | Eligible for penalty claims. |

### Application Format 1: For Failed Transaction at Your Home Bank ATM

Use this written application format if you used your own bank's ATM (e.g., you hold an SBI account and used an SBI ATM).

> **To,**
> **The Branch Manager,**
> [Name of Your Bank, e.g., State Bank of India]
> [Branch Name & Full Address]
> [City, State, PIN Code]
>
> **Date:** [Insert Date of Writing Letter]
>
> **SUBJECT: Application for refund of Rs. [Amount] debited due to failed ATM transaction (ATM Cash Not Dispensed)**
>
> **Respected Sir/Madam,**
>
> I am writing to formally bring to your attention a failed cash withdrawal transaction that occurred on my savings account maintained at your branch.
>
> The details of my bank account and debit card are as follows:
> * **Account Holder Name:** [Your Full Legal Name]
> * **Savings Account Number:** [Your Account Number]
> * **Debit Card Number:** [First 4 and Last 4 digits, e.g., 4591 XXXX XXXX 1234]
> * **Registered Mobile Number:** [+91-XXXXXXXXXX]
>
> On **[Date of Transaction]** at approximately **[Time, e.g., 02:45 PM]**, I attempted to withdraw an amount of **Rs. [Amount in Figures]/-** from your bank's ATM located at **[Exact Location/Address of the ATM Kiosk]**.
>
> Although the transaction was processed and the requested amount of Rs. [Amount] was debited from my account (as confirmed via SMS alert), **the ATM machine did not dispense any physical cash**. 
>
> The details of the failed transaction are listed below:
> * **ATM Identification Number (ATM ID):** [Insert ATM ID if known]
> * **Transaction Slip Number / RRN:** [Insert RRN/TXN Number]
> * **Exact Amount Debited:** Rs. [Amount in Figures]/-
>
> As per the directives issued by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), banks are required to auto-reverse failed ATM transactions within T+5 days. Therefore, I request you to kindly investigate the electronic journal logs / cash balance of the specified ATM and credit the debited amount of **Rs. [Amount]/-** back to my savings account at the earliest.
>
> I have attached a photocopy of the ATM transaction receipt and my bank passbook/statement highlighting the unauthorized debit for your quick reference.
>
> Thanking you.
>
> **Yours faithfully,**
>
> *(Signature)*
> **[Your Full Name]**
> **[Your Full Postal Address]**
> **[Contact Number]**
> **[Email Address]**
>
> **Enclosures:**
> 1. Copy of ATM Transaction Slip / Screenshot of SMS alert.
> 2. Copy of Bank Passbook / Statement highlighting the debit entry.
> 3. Copy of Debit Card (Front side only).

> * **ATM Terminal ID:** [Insert ATM ID from receipt or screen]
> * **Transaction Reference Number (RRN):** [Insert 12-digit RRN]

>

>
> I request you to kindly take up this matter with the acquiring bank via the National Financial Switch (NFS) network reconciliation process and arrange for an immediate credit of **Rs. [Amount]/-** to my account. Furthermore, if the resolution exceeds the statutory 5-day period, I request the applicable compensation of Rs. 100 per day as mandated by RBI regulations.
>
> Thank you for your prompt assistance.
>
> **Yours sincerely,**
>
> *(Signature)*

> **[Your Account Number]**
> **[Mobile Number]**
