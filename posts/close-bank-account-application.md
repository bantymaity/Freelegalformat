Let’s be honest. Opening a bank account in India is incredibly easy—sometimes just a matter of a few clicks and a video KYC. But closing one? That is a completely different story. 

Most of us end up with multiple bank accounts over the years. Maybe you switched jobs, relocated to a new city, or just got tempted by a credit card offer. Eventually, keeping track of them becomes a headache. Worse, those "Minimum Average Balance" (MAB) non-maintenance charges quietly eat away your hard-earned money month after month. 

When you finally decide to shut an inactive account down, the bank staff often hands you a blank sheet of paper and tells you to "write an application." If you are sitting in a crowded bank branch wondering exactly what to write, you are in the right place. 

Here is everything you need to know to close your bank account smoothly, without making multiple trips to the branch.

### 3 Things You MUST Do Before Closing Your Account

Do not just walk into the bank and hand over a closure letter. If you miss these preliminary steps, your application will likely be rejected.

1. **De-link Your Investments and EMIs:** Is your Netflix subscription, mutual fund SIP, home loan EMI, or EPF linked to this account? If yes, immediately update your new bank details on those platforms. An account cannot be closed if there is a pending auto-debit mandate.
2. **Download Your Statements:** Once your account is closed, you will lose access to your net banking and mobile app. Download your bank statements for at least the last 2 to 3 years. You will need them for filing Income Tax Returns (ITR) or applying for visas in the future.
3. **Empty the Account (Almost):** Transfer your funds to your active account, but leave a small balance (around ₹500 to ₹1000). Why? Because banks often charge an "Account Closure Fee" if you close the account between 14 days and 1 year of opening it. Leaving a small balance ensures they can deduct their fee and hand you the rest in cash.

### How to Write an Application for Closing a Bank Account

Writing letter is simple. Keep it short, professional, and directly to the point. You need to mention your account number, the reason for closing, and how you want your remaining balance paid out.

Here are two ready-to-use formats. Just copy the one that fits your situation, fill in your details, and take a printout.

#### Format 1: Standard Savings Account Closure Letter
Use this general format if you simply do not need the account anymore due to multiple accounts or minimum balance issues.

> **To,**
> **The Branch Manager,**
> [Name of the Bank, e.g., State Bank of India]
> [Branch Name / Address]
> 
> **Date:** [Insert Date]
> 
> **Subject: Application for Closing Savings Bank Account.**
> 
> **Reference:** Account Number [Your 11 to 15 digit Account Number]
> 
> Respected Sir/Madam,
> 
> I, [Your Full Name], have been holding a savings bank account at your branch for the past [Number] years. The account number is [Your Account Number]. 
> 
> Due to some personal reasons, I am currently maintaining multiple bank accounts and find it difficult to manage this one. Therefore, I request you to kindly close this savings account with immediate effect. 
> 
> I have surrendered my unused chequebook, debit card, and passbook to the bank along with this application. 
> 
> Kindly transfer the remaining balance of my account to my alternate bank account. The details are as follows:
> *   **Account Name:** [Your Name]
> *   **Account Number:** [Your Other Bank Account Number]
> *   **IFSC Code:** [IFSC Code]
> *   **Bank Name:** [Other Bank Name]
> 
> I have enclosed a copy of my Aadhaar card and PAN card for your reference. Kindly process the closure at the earliest.
> 
> Thanking you,
> 
> **Yours faithfully,**
> 
> [Your Signature matching the bank records]
> 
> **Name:** [Your Full Name]
> **Mobile Number:** [Your Registered Mobile Number]

#### Format 2: Account Closure Due to Relocation / Job Transfer
If you are moving to a new city and prefer closing the account rather than transferring the home branch, use this format.

> **To,**
> **The Branch Manager,**
> [Name of the Bank]
> [Branch Name / Address]
> 
> **Date:** [Insert Date]
> 
> **Subject: Request for Account Closure due to Relocation.**
> 
> Dear Sir/Madam,
> 
> This is to inform you that I, [Your Full Name], hold a savings account (Account Number: [Your Account Number]) with your branch. 
> 
> I am permanently relocating to another city for professional reasons, and it will no longer be possible for me to operate this account efficiently. Hence, I humbly request you to close my account. 
> 
> Please deduct any applicable account closure charges from my existing balance and hand over the remaining amount to me in cash / issue a Demand Draft / transfer it to my new account [Provide details if choosing transfer].
> 
> All the bank-issued materials, including the ATM card and chequebook, have been attached to this letter for destruction.
> 
> Thank you for the excellent service provided during my tenure with your branch.
> 
> **Warm regards,**
> 
> [Your Signature]
> 
> **Name:** [Your Full Name]
> **Mobile Number:** [Your Registered Mobile Number]

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

**Can I close my bank account online?**
In India, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) mandates strict KYC norms. Because of this, most major banks do not allow you to close a savings or current account entirely online. You or your authorized representative must visit the home branch physically to sign the closure form and surrender your debit cards.

**Will the bank charge me for closing the account?**
It depends on when you are closing it. If you close the account within 14 days of opening it, there are zero charges. If you close it between 14 days and 1 year, banks usually charge a fee ranging from ₹300 to ₹500 plus GST. If you close it after 1 year, most banks do not charge any closure fee.

**What should I carry to the bank?**
Do not forget to take your original Aadhaar and PAN card, a self-attested photocopy of both, your current debit card (ATM card), passbook, and any unused cheque leaves. The bank official will physically cut your debit card and chequebook in half in front of you.
