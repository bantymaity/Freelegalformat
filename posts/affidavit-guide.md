An affidavit is a sworn statement of facts written down and signed by the person making it (called the deponent) before a person authorized to administer oaths, such as a notary public or an oath commissioner.

### Common Uses of an Affidavit in India
1. **Name Change:** If you want to officially change your name in documents, an affidavit is the very first step.
2. **Address Proof:** Often used when you don't have a standard address proof for a new rental place.
3. **Gap Certificate:** Required by students to explain a gap year in their education.
4. **Income Proof:** Sometimes required for government schemes or school admissions.

### How to Create One?
Draft the content clearly, print it on a Non-Judicial Stamp Paper of the required value (usually ₹20 to ₹100 depending on your state), and sign it in front of a Notary Public.
